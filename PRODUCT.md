# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Virtual Agency Studio is used by a single local operator running an AI influencer character studio. The operator is in a production workflow: shaping character identity, generating image concepts, reviewing assets, approving drafts, recording publishing outcomes, and deciding when automation may change canon, memory, or constitution.

## Product Purpose

The product manages AI influencer characters through observable, human-approved automation. Success means the operator can see what every run did, what needs review, which assets and drafts are ready, and where identity changes require approval. The central primitive is a Run, and every automated action should leave readable events, artifacts, decisions, and review gates.

## Brand Personality

Disciplined, editorial, technical. The interface should feel like a stylish model agency operating desk: quiet, ordered, neutral, creative, and precise. It should express taste through composition, proportion, and restraint, not decorative noise or explanatory copy.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, AI-purple gradients, playful startup clutter, oversized marketing heroes, cute assistant language, heavy card stacks, and modal-first workflows. Avoid interfaces that hide operator control or make automation feel magical instead of inspectable. The UI should not read as a consumer social app, a CRM, a crypto terminal, or a cosmetic redesign over weak flow.

## Design Principles

1. Flow before decoration: the next meaningful operator action should be obvious from layout and hierarchy.
2. Agency discipline: use grids, editorial spacing, restrained color, and model-agency calm to make complex workflows feel composed.
3. Human approval is visible: publishing, canon, constitution, provider overrides, and identity evolution must show their review state plainly.
4. Automation is observable: runs, events, provider jobs, artifacts, and decisions should remain readable without exposing unsafe local paths or secrets.
5. Minimal words, strong structure: reduce explanatory UI copy and let labels, grouping, and progressive detail communicate the system.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to WCAG 2.2 AA contrast, keyboard-operable controls, visible focus states, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts without horizontal overflow. Motion should communicate state changes only. Avoid relying on color alone for status, and keep dense operational views readable on mobile and desktop.
